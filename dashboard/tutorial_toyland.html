You've got a toy distribution business. Stock it. Ship it. Sell it. Do it better than the baseline and the leaderboard is yours. This page gets you from zero to running bot in under 20 minutes.

01 The game

What your bot is actually doing.

You run ToyLand Distribution Co. — 1 warehouse, 4 physical stores (Bangkok, Chiang Mai, Phuket), 2 online channels, and 12 product categories across 78 SKUs. Every simulated day, customers walk in, products sell, stock depletes. Your bot decides:

  • What to order from suppliers, and when
  • How to move stock from the warehouse to stores (and between stores)
  • What goes on the A-shelves (high-visibility, 1.8× conversion boost)
  • Whether to discount slow movers (optional)

The sim runs 12 to 60 months of business in seconds. At the end you see revenue, profit, stockout rate, and shelf efficiency. Your goal: beat the baseline bot.

03 Your bot, minimally

Here's the smallest thing that works.

Every bot is a Python class with four public-info attributes and one decide() method. Each sim day, decide() gets the current state and returns a list of actions.

class MyBot:
    def __init__(self):
        self.catalog = {}           # pid → product info
        self.suppliers = []
        self.locations = []
        self.physical_locs = []
        self.product_supplier = {}  # pid → best supplier_id

    def decide(self, state):
        # state["stock"] = {(loc_id, pid): qty}
        # state["date"], state["day"], state["cash"], etc.
        commands = []

        # Example: reorder every product whose WH stock is low
        for pid, prod in self.catalog.items():
            wh_stock = state["stock"].get(("WH-01", pid), 0)
            if wh_stock < 30:
                commands.append({
                    "action": "issue_po",
                    "supplier_id": self.product_supplier[pid],
                    "product_id": pid,
                    "qty": 100,
                })

        return commands
HEADS UP   Your bot file cannot import subprocess, socket, requests, or call eval/exec/open. The sandbox blocks these. Full list: see engine/server.py on GitHub.

04 What actions you can take

Return any mix of these from decide().

issue_poOrder from supplier
Ship qty of product_id from supplier_id to WH-01. Arrives after the supplier's lead time (5–10 days typical). Reliability may partially fulfill.
transferMove stock
Move qty of product_id from from_loc to to_loc. Bangkok: 1 day. Upcountry: 2–3 days. Online stores fulfill from WH-01 directly.
set_discountCut the price
discount_pct is 0–1 (0.10 = 10% off). Omit location_id for global. Customers respond to discounts via a price-elasticity model.
set_shelfRearrange shelves
Place product_id on shelf_grade (A/B/C) at location_id. A-shelves give 1.8× demand weight. Used to be the Champion bot's secret sauce.

05 Upload and run

Ship it.

  • Save your bot as my_bot.py
  • Open the Play page → BizBot Bash tab → Submit Bot
  • Upload the file. The server sandboxes it, registers it, and drops it into the Mode dropdown

  • Compare against Demo Baseline, Smart, Champion, Ai Genius
